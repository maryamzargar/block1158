BLOCK 1158

In the late 1950’s the area from 60th to 65th Streets between Amsterdam Avenue and the 11th Avenue railroad tracks, was know as the San Juan Hill. It was largely an Afro-American and Puerto Rican neighborhood. San Juan Hill was one of the largest and poorest African-American neighborhoods in Manhattan before the rise of Harlem. It was at this time that the African-American population exodus to Harlem and a wave of Puerto Rican residents moved in. The gang activities and the poor conditions of the neighborhood inspired Leonard Bernstein and Stephen Sondheim to write the popular West Side Story in the late 1950’s. In 1955 John D. Rockefeller 3rd, one of the principal contributors, began with funds for planning of Lincoln Center.
